Casual Dinner & Drinks (City/East/South)

Winghaus, Barangaroo

📍 Shop 1, Scotch Row, Hickson Road, Barangaroo · Wings, beer & good chaos

Loud, fun and very “after work exhale”. Wings, burgers, beer towers and lots of screens. Great for relaxed catch‑ups where the food is easy and the vibe is casual.

🔗 Website & bookings

Barangaroo House

📍 35 Barangaroo Ave, Barangaroo · Views + varied menus

Multi‑level venue on the water with different moods on each floor. Good for something a bit nicer while still relaxed – sharing plates, drinks and “oh right, Sydney is pretty” views.

🔗 Website & bookings

Chin Chin Sydney

📍 69 Commonwealth St, Surry Hills · Modern Asian, buzzy

Big energy, share plates, lots of flavour. Good if you want “fun night out” without being overly formal. Popular, so worth having a booking.

🔗 Website & bookings

Nomad

📍 16 Foster St, Surry Hills · Wine, wood‑fired, grown‑up

Beautiful space, wood‑fired dishes, strong wine list. Ideal for a slightly more polished dinner that still feels warm and relaxed.

🔗 Website & bookings

Coogee Pavilion

📍 169 Dolphin St, Coogee · Beachy, multi‑level, very “east”

Big, bright, and right on the beach. Pizza, seafood, rooftop bar – very good for a weekend evening or long casual dinner with a bit of people‑watching.

🔗 Website & bookings

Bakeries & Sweet Things (City/East/South)

Bourke Street Bakery

📍 Surry Hills, Barangaroo & more · Classic Sydney bakery energy

Sausage rolls, tarts, crusty bread, good coffee. Perfect for a grab‑and‑go snack or “I need something comforting and flaky” between meetings.

🔗 Website

Flour and Stone

📍 53 Riley St, Woolloomooloo · Cake, lamingtons & very good vibes

Known for lamingtons and beautiful cakes. Cosy and indulgent – ideal for a “treat yourself” afternoon tea or picking up something nice to share.

🔗 Website

Black Star Pastry

📍 Newtown, Rosebery & CBD · Famous strawberry watermelon cake

Iconic for a reason – light, pretty cakes, good coffee, and a very Sydney feel. Good option if you want something visually impressive as well as tasty.

🔗 Website

Brickfields

📍 Chippendale & Marrickville · Coffee + excellent baked things

Slightly tucked away but loved by locals. Smaller space, but very good bread and pastries.

🔗 Website

Sweet Belem

📍 Petersham · Portuguese tarts heaven (southern-ish inner west)

A little further south/inner‑west, but their pastéis de nata are worth the trip. Great if you want to bring something special to share.

🔗 Website

Street Food, Markets & Food‑Hall Vibes

Tramsheds

📍 1 Dalgal Way, Forest Lodge · Indoor market vibe, casual eats

Mix of casual vendors under one roof – pizza, ramen, gelato, wine bars. Great when everyone wants something different and you don’t feel like negotiating.

🔗 Website

The Rocks Markets & Food Trucks

📍 The Rocks · Weekends / events

Rotating food stalls and trucks with a view of the Harbour Bridge. Fun for a wander, snacks and a bit of “I live in a postcard” energy.

🔗 Market info

Spice Alley

📍 Kensington St, Chippendale · Outdoor Asian street‑food laneway

Lanterns, hawker‑style food, casual seating. Easy to grab different dishes and share. Great for “walk in, see what smells good, order that.”

🔗 Website

Darling Harbour / Darling Quarter kiosks

📍 Darling Harbour · Waterfront casual, family‑friendly

A mix of casual spots dotted along the water – burgers, noodles, gelato. Good for low‑key evenings with an easy walk and people‑watching.

🔗 List of venues

Entertainment Quarter & Moore Park events

📍 Moore Park · Event‑driven food truck options

Around big events (games, concerts, festivals) the EQ and Moore Park often have food trucks and pop‑ups. Not a fixed list, but worth keeping in mind on event nights.

Breakfast & Coffee (City/East/South)

Edition Coffee Roasters

📍 Barangaroo & Haymarket · Nordic‑Japanese inspired café

Beautiful coffee and slightly fancy breakfast options with a Nordic‑Japanese twist. Great for a pre‑work catch‑up that feels a little bit special.

🔗 Website

Single O

📍 Surry Hills & CBD · Coffee‑nerd heaven

One for coffee people. Great brews, simple food, lots of energy. Ideal if you need caffeine plus somewhere to debrief the week.

🔗 Cafés

Three Blue Ducks (Brontë / Rosebery)

📍 Brontë & Rosebery · Brunch with a bit of polish

Solid brunch options with a focus on good produce. Nice if you want something that feels like “going out for breakfast” rather than just grabbing a coffee.

🔗 Website & bookings

Reuben Hills

📍 61 Albion St, Surry Hills · Café/roastery with a cult following

Great coffee, inventive brunch plates, very Surry Hills. Good for a weekend morning or slightly longer weekday breakfast.

🔗 Website

XS Espresso (Multiple locations incl. Mascot)

📍 Mascot & more · Over‑the‑top shakes & brunch

If you want something a bit extra (think big shakes, loaded waffles, etc.) this is a fun option in the south.

🔗 Website
