Today's Headlines (April 5, 2026)

Economy & Cost of Living:

  • Grocery Price Hike: Australians are facing up to 20% increases in meat, fruit, and vegetable prices due to Middle East conflict impacting supply chains. The government is referring this to the ACCC and doubling price gouging penalties.
  • Fuel Security: The government is actively seeking supply guarantees from Asian partners (Singapore, South Korea, Malaysia, Japan) with Australia importing 90% of its refined fuel.
  • Pension Boost: Good news for pensioners—Centrelink confirms payments up to $1,178 per fortnight for April 2026 to combat rising living costs.

Environment & Natural Disasters:

  • Murray-Darling Crisis: Invasive species are threatening the vital Murray-Darling Basin, pushing the waterway towards collapse.
  • Earthquakes & Cyclones: A magnitude 6.01 earthquake struck the Northern Territory (no casualties), and Tropical Cyclone Maila is rapidly intensifying in the Solomon Sea, with a potential threat to North Queensland.

Crime & Public Safety:

  • Road Fatalities: Multiple fatal road incidents across Queensland and South Australia highlight calls for patience and increased police presence over the Easter period.
  • SA Arrests: Three arrests in Elizabeth Vale, SA, following a stolen car pursuit, and police are investigating a suspicious grass fire at Kuitpo.
  • AFP Vehicle Damage: A WA man is charged with damaging a federal police vehicle at Perth Airport.

Sports:

  • Surfing Upset: Eight-time world champion Stephanie Gilmore was knocked out in the opening round at Bells Beach by rising star Luana Silva.

Other Notable Mentions:

  • ABC Breached Code: An episode of ABC’s “Four Corners” on Northern Territory water usage was found to breach accuracy and impartiality codes.
  • Australian Modernism: A new exhibition showcases Australian Modernism with works from the Heide collection and Mirka Mora estate.
  • Bilby Comeback: A breeding trial in Mallee Cliffs National Park is successfully reintroducing bilbies, with the population growing to almost 2,000.
