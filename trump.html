Donald Trump

Businessman, media personality and 45th president of the United States.

Donald Trump at a formal event, pictured speaking in front of an ornate backdrop. Source: personal reference image provided by Drew.

Early life and business career

Donald John Trump was born on June 14, 1946, in Queens, New York City. He studied economics at the Wharton School of the University of Pennsylvania and joined his family's real‑estate business, which he later renamed the Trump Organization. Over several decades he became widely known for developing and branding real‑estate, hotel and casino projects, as well as for licensing the Trump name across a range of ventures.

Trump gained broader public recognition as a media figure, particularly through his role as host and executive producer of the reality television series The Apprentice, which first aired in 2004. His persona as a decisive, high‑profile businessman was a central part of his public image long before he entered electoral politics.

Political rise and presidency

Trump announced his candidacy for president of the United States in 2015 as a Republican. He focused on themes including immigration, trade, public safety and a "America First" approach to foreign and economic policy. In the 2016 election he won the presidency, defeating Democratic nominee Hillary Clinton, and served as the 45th president from January 20, 2017, to January 20, 2021.

His time in office was marked by significant policy changes and notable appointments, including tax legislation, deregulation initiatives, a different approach to international agreements and alliances, and the nomination of several Supreme Court justices. His presidency was also characterised by strong partisan division, an unconventional communication style, and a heavy use of social media.

Post‑presidency and ongoing influence

After leaving office in 2021, Trump has remained a prominent and polarising figure in American public life. He has continued to influence political debates, endorse candidates and hold rallies for supporters. His business ventures and public profile, along with ongoing legal and political developments, continue to attract extensive media coverage and public discussion.
